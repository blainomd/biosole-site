Gait Biomarkers
Gait as the 8th Vital Sign

Six biomarkers.
One insole.

Research links gait speed to cognitive decline, fall risk, cardiovascular health, and surgical outcomes. BioSole captures all of it passively.

Gait Speed
The single strongest predictor of health outcomes in adults over 60. Below 0.8 m/s correlates with 3x hospitalization risk within 12 months.
Clinical threshold: <0.8 m/s = high risk
Step Symmetry
Asymmetry greater than 10% between left and right indicates compensatory loading — a leading indicator of musculoskeletal injury or surgical complications.
Normal: >92% bilateral symmetry
Ground Contact Time
Prolonged contact time signals reduced propulsion force. Trending upward over weeks is a sensitive early warning for sarcopenia and fall risk.
Normal: 550–680 ms per step
Cadence
Steps per minute, normalized by height and age. Below 100 spm in community-dwelling adults correlates with falls in 12-month prospective studies.
Target: 100–120 spm for adults 65+
Pronation Index
Medial-lateral pressure distribution reveals overpronation or supination patterns. Directly flags orthotic compliance for post-surgical patients.
Neutral: ±12° from midstance
Fall Risk Score
ML composite of gait velocity variability, step width, and center-of-pressure trajectory. Validated against 18-month fall incidence in 2,400 patients.
Elevated: score >40/100
